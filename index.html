introduction

Uganda is endowed with numerous aquatic resources with aqua cultural potential, it has highly varied climatic and geographic regions, covering apart the largest portion of the fresh water in the world Lake Victoria and several rivers, swamps and other wetlands all of which supports an abundance of native aquatic species and of which many can sustainably contribute to the construction of pond or cleared like the one of Nabisojjo for fish production.

Nakaseke District is an agricultural district with an estimated population of 419,700 People (2009) and the majority are peasants. The district owns one minor lake/natural water bodies Nabisojjo.
Supply of fish and fish products are limited to the community for consumption, shortage and demand for fish and animal protein is at an increasing rate yet about 80% of the population is living in poor and rural conditions and cannot afford fish due to high prices and export demand.

company profile

Today in Uganda the market for fish is constantly increasing, the demand is high which convinced us to invest in aquaculture since most of the water bodies in the country are good and favorable for aquaculture.
Nabisojjo fish farm limited have established a fish farm in the waters of Nabisojjo minor lake of free range, cages and ponds.
Lake Nabisojjo is located in Wakyato Sub-county, Nakaseke District on Kampala Ngoma road the project is actually on the right side of the road before reaching Nabisojjo Trading Centre in a distance of 90 kilometers from Kampala.

It is categorized as a minor lake (FIRI) with an area of 5-6 square kilometers. However the company did identified one part of lake with an estimated area of 388.5 hectares of water surface. technology under consideration

Semi extensive fish farming appropriate artificial propagation of clarias gariepinus cat fish and Tilapia are advantageous over natural and semi-natural fry production / breeding. It has been projected for commercial fry production and its advantage is that production, planning for quality, quantity and reliable fry is guaranteed and farmers will be supplied with fingerling which will be nursed by the project to at least with a body weight of 50-80grams of which in the project’s research most farmers their failure was found that they were supplied with fry 0.5grm-1grm which led them to straggle much to feed them regarding to feeds provided to them had been finished.

According to their explanation (farmers) they were trained and advised to prepare feeds at their own farms but not given when and at what size of the fish should be fed on. The self processed feeds led to poor growth of the fry supplied to farmers even some sought they were supplied with poor quality where as it was from Kajansi station.
The company has come to save the situation by supplying the farmers with fingerlings of the size which survive and even accept to feed on farmer’s self processed feeds however it may cause delay in terms of harvest but the farm can’t fail to benefit from a cycle.

Here farmers will be given fingerling worth his/her stocking capacity of the pond and they will not be advice to inter crop with catfish as have been recommended previously to control the population in the pond, the company through its coordinators as the stocked fish begin to breed the company will be collecting the new born fry back to the project’s farm for nursing to preferable size for the next seeding to its members and also the extra to be supplied to other farmers this will create consistence and sustainable supply of fingerlings to farmers all the time.

social and environmental context to the project

The project area is Wakyato Sub-county/Nakaseke District which is largely a rural settling with a few urban centers. Fish farming is not new to the district; it has been there since 1954 after the establishment of Kajjansi aquaculture center in Uganda. Fish farming was done at a subsistence level without good technology and poor knowledge. The district is endowed with wetlands, spring wells, loamy clay soils, potential for water harvest for production and domestic use. There is also a high and well established culture for eating fish. All the above are good for aquaculture, horticultural crops and livestock farming. The project will utilize such land for fish farming without degrading as well as encouraging and training farmer how they can benefit from wetlands and conservation of the environment.

potential to general social and economical impact

The project will bring together communities with a possibility of forming associations/groups hence creation of fore for strengthening themselves, expression of their interests and aspirations which will intertwine the project into the social-economical fabric of the society. Integration of fish farming will translate into increased production per unit area of land and diversification of farm produces hence poverty reduction.

The project will expose the members to participatory process of managing profitable and enterprises of mixed farming.

The fish is being feed on pellet feeds bought from Ugachick which is costing the project too much which is at 1550 to 2500/ per acres, we propose that if we could get our own pelletize machine it could make us save 30% from the commercial complete pellet feeds.
The Pelletize machine is at Shs. 12 000 000/= and the advantage with it is that it’s a diesel driven machine. Pellet feeds needed for the nixed six will be 120 bags of 30kgs each of 35% protein content of size 2mm this will be for the newly born fingering which is 3600kgs at 76 000/= per bag for starter and growers .
And 240 bags of 30kgs each bag of 25% protein content of size 5mm which is 7200kgs at 46 500/= per bag for grow out and brood stock this will cost the project 20 280 000/= (twenty million two hundred eighty thousand shillings).
In the process of feeding the fish, during the research it have been proved that tilapia do eat greens leaves like of cassava, potato, cabbage and whatever chicken could eat, as of now the project is getting the greens from the community around the project.

Here the project have started encouraging the community to start growing cassava and potato for both leaves harvesting for the project as well as getting food for their consumption. At this point the management is encouraging the community to grow bigger gardens so as to be able to sell more leaves to the project and also to create food security for their consumption, the project is helping the community by opening land for them and also supplying them with stems and potato leaves for planting, the sector is named leave trade.
The project has identified cassava and potato spices with more leaves from NARO. Provided leaves are collected in plenty from the project’s sambas and the community’s this will save the project 30% of pellet feeds needed for the project.
